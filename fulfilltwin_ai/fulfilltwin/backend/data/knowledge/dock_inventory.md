# Dock congestion
Prioritize trailers by departure commitment, replenishment criticality, dwell time, and available door capability. Hold low-value yard moves when dock congestion threatens time-critical inbound or outbound flow.

# Inventory shortages
Protect replenishment for high-velocity items and orders closest to service failure. Suppress release of work with low inventory confidence when doing so prevents repeated shorts and rework.

# Coordination
Dock, inventory, and production decisions must be coordinated. Accelerating inbound flow without available receiving capacity can worsen congestion, while accelerating outbound production without trailer capacity can move the bottleneck rather than remove it.
