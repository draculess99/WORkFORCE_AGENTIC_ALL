# GridGuard Operations Playbook

## Human authority
GridGuard is advisory software. A qualified operator must approve, reject, or modify every recommendation. The application must never claim that demand response, dispatch, switching, or emergency action was executed.

## Reserve-margin interpretation
A positive reserve margin indicates that assumed available capacity exceeds forecast peak demand. Thin margins require progressively closer monitoring because forecast error, outages, weather changes, and transfer limits can remove the apparent cushion. A negative reserve margin is a capacity-deficit scenario and requires immediate escalation to the grid-operations lead.

## Escalation sequence
For normal conditions, maintain the standard refresh cycle. For watch conditions, validate resource availability and prepare voluntary demand response. For elevated conditions, activate targeted demand-response preparation, confirm fast-start resources, and schedule operator checkpoints. For critical conditions, escalate to the operations lead, validate transfer capability and contingency resources, and prepare emergency measures under approved procedures.

## Decision record
Every human decision should record the forecast version, risk level, reserve margin, scenario assumptions, recommendation, operator rationale, reviewer, and timestamp. Rejections and overrides are as important as approvals.
