# Demand-Response Policy Notes

Demand response should be proportional to the forecast risk and limited to the minimum action necessary. Voluntary programs and targeted reductions should be considered before emergency measures when time and conditions allow.

A recommendation should identify the expected peak window, required reduction or contingency gap, uncertainty, and the next review time. It should not imply that customers, generators, or control devices have been contacted unless an external operational system confirms that action.

When several high-risk hours are consecutive, the plan should cover the full stress interval rather than only the single highest point. When an outage scenario is active, capacity must be reduced before calculating reserve margin.
